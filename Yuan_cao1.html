Research and Internship Experience

Design of an Intelligent Elevator Measurement and Control System

Description:

Engaging in this research endeavor, I engaged in the development of an 8-storey elevator analog control system based on STM32F103C8T6 chip, and realizes the method of contactless elevator control based on single chip microcomputer.

Experiences:

  1. Conceptualized and meticulously designed various circuit modules, playing a pivotal role in regulating the elevator's multifaceted operations.

  2. Innovatively introduced features such as voice announcements and image display functions, elevating user interaction and usability.

  3. Executed comprehensive simulations utilizing Altium Designer software and proficiently employed the C programming language to craft the central control program.

Development of an Electronic Nose for Environmental Monitoring Applications

Description:

During the course of this internship, I undertook a focused exploration of optimizing device sensitivity by systematically adjusting diverse parameters, leading to the identification of optimal values within a reasonable range through an iterative process of continuous experimentation.

Experiences:

  1. Analyzed data from sensors collected in different environmental conditions and with varying structures, contributing to a deeper understanding of sensor performance and behavior.

  2. Proficiently utilized COMSOL simulations to assess and validate the device's performance across various scenarios, advancing the mastery of simulation tools for enhanced experimentation.

Embedded System for Prosthetic Applications(ESPA)

Description:

ESPA is a small-form microelectronic signal processing device developed for mobile BCI applications. I engaged in the development of the Bluetooth module and leveraging Redis to empower remote and rapid configuration of distinct modules for ESPA.

Achievements:

  1. Successfully established preliminary HID services using the RN4870 Bluetooth chip, enabling mouse movement control in the Android environment.

  2. Implemented remote Bluetooth control through Redis streams, allowing for ESPA's initialization via input YAML files and dynamic modification of Bluetooth runtime parameters.
